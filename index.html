About

Public servant and strategist with expertise in public policy and cybersecurity. Life coach, content creator and musician exploring the digital landscape — writing music and verse under the takhallus Khalil Asgar (ख़लील अस्गर).

From Village Anjorpur, District Ballia, Uttar Pradesh, Bharat — PIN 277502 — currently posted in Rourkela, Odisha. I serve in the Indian Telecommunication Service under the Department of Telecommunications, Government of India — while writing songs, building small automations, and exploring the long Indian conversation about knowing, unknowing and movement.

The name of this place — netineti charaiveti charaiveti — stitches together two ancient Sanskrit ideas: neti neti (the via negativa of the Upanishads, "not this, not this") and charaiveti from the Aitareya Brahmana ("keep moving, keep moving"). Disciplined doubt + relentless motion.

04

Wisdom

Neti, neti

The Upanishadic discipline of negation. Whatever you can point to and name — that is not it. A practice of release, not of accumulation.

Charaiveti

The Aitareya verse to the wanderer: a sitting fortune sits, a standing one stands, a sleeping one sleeps, but a moving one finds honey. Charan vai madhu vindati.

The synthesis

Disciplined unknowing paired with relentless motion. The two together are a posture for a life of public service, creative work, and quiet inquiry.
